About Reading Speeds

How to determine your reading speed:

  • Track how long it takes you to read a few books
  • Divide the word count by the number of days it took
  • Average across multiple books for better accuracy

Typical reading speeds:

  • Ebook: 12,000-20,000 words/day
  • Physical books: 10,000-15,000 words/day
  • Audiobooks: 20,000-30,000 words/day (depends on playback speed)

Note: These settings only affect future estimates. Existing readings with manual overrides will not be changed.

Reading Speed

The metrics row at the bottom of the EPUB reader (WPM, average seconds per page, chapter / book time remaining) is driven by a rolling buffer of recent page-turn samples. Here's what's in that buffer and how it's filtered.

What gets measured: each page turn records { ms: time on page, words: words-per-page }. words comes from Calibre's #word_count column divided by the current paginated page count; if a book has no word count the reader falls back to averaging raw per-page time. The per-page timer pauses when the WebView is hidden, so background time isn't charged to the page.

Stage 1 — absolute window (insert-time): any sample with ms outside the Minimum/Maximum seconds-per-page window configured above is dropped at the page turn and never enters the buffer. This catches accidental swipes and phone-down time.

Stage 2 — MAD-based outlier rejection (compute-time): at every metrics tick, buffered samples are filtered against the buffer's own distribution with a robust modified-z-score test:

M = 0.6745 · (x − median) / MAD
where MAD = median(|xᵢ − median|)

Reject the sample if  |M| > 3.5

This is the Iglewicz & Hoaglin test (threshold ≈ p < 0.001). MAD is used instead of standard deviation because the deviation is itself inflated by the outliers you're trying to detect — the median and MAD barely move when a single anomalous value appears. The stage-2 filter is a pass-through until the buffer holds at least 5 samples.

Why ms-per-word, not raw ms: when a word count is available Stage 2 filters on ms / words, so toggling dual-page mode or changing font size (both of which change words-per-page) doesn't make steady reading look anomalous. The displayed average projects ms-per-word back to ms-per-page using the current words-per-page.

Buffer mechanics: FIFO ring of the last 30 samples (~15–30 min of reading), persisted in progress.json per book so a fresh install resumes your calibrated speed. EPUB only — PDFs don't populate this.

Derived metrics:

WPM            = 60000 / msPerWord
avg sec/page   = msPerWord × words-per-page  (or raw mean ms)
book remain    = (totalPages − pageIndex) × avg
chapter remain = (chapterEnd − pageIndex) × avg
